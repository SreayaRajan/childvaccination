Child Vaccination Information

Vaccines Schedule

  • At Birth

    Hepatitis B Vaccine (HBV): This vaccine protects against the hepatitis B virus, which can cause serious liver infection. It is crucial to receive this vaccine as a single dose at birth to provide early protection.

  • 2 Months

    DTP Vaccine: Protects against diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis. Diphtheria can cause severe throat infections, tetanus leads to muscle stiffness, and whooping cough (pertussis) can be fatal in infants. This is the first dose.

    IPV (Inactivated Polio Vaccine): This vaccine protects against poliomyelitis, a debilitating disease that can cause paralysis. The first dose is given to ensure early immunity.

    Hib (Haemophilus influenzae type b): Protects against severe bacterial infections that can lead to meningitis and pneumonia. The first dose helps shield infants from these serious illnesses.

    Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV): This vaccine protects against pneumonia, meningitis, and other infections caused by the pneumococcal bacteria. Administering the first dose early is essential for protection.

    Rotavirus Vaccine: Protects against severe diarrhea caused by rotavirus, a common virus in young children. Early vaccination is crucial for preventing dehydration and hospitalization.

  • 4 Months

    DTP Vaccine: Second dose to reinforce immunity against diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis, ensuring continued protection.

    IPV: Second dose to maintain protection against poliomyelitis.

    Hib: Second dose to further protect against severe bacterial infections.

    PCV: Second dose to boost immunity against pneumonia and related diseases.

    Rotavirus Vaccine: Second dose to enhance protection against severe diarrhea.

  • 6 Months

    DTP Vaccine: Third dose to ensure long-lasting immunity against diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis.

    Hepatitis B Vaccine: Second dose, which is important for sustaining protection against hepatitis B virus.

  • 12-15 Months

    MMR Vaccine: This vaccine protects against measles, mumps, and rubella, three highly contagious diseases. The first dose is vital for establishing immunity.

    Varicella Vaccine: Protects against chickenpox, a highly contagious viral infection. The first dose helps to significantly reduce the risk of infection.

    Hib: Booster dose to ensure ongoing protection against serious bacterial infections.

    PCV: Booster dose to enhance protection against pneumococcal diseases.

  • 15-18 Months

    DTP Vaccine: Booster dose to reinforce immunity against diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis, ensuring children remain protected as they grow.

  • 4-6 Years

    MMR Vaccine: Second dose to provide lasting immunity against measles, mumps, and rubella.

    Varicella Vaccine: Second dose to solidify protection against chickenpox.

    DTP Vaccine: Booster dose to maintain immunity against diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis.

    IPV: Booster dose to continue protection against poliomyelitis.

  • 11-12 Years

    Tdap Vaccine: This booster protects against tetanus, diphtheria, and pertussis. It's crucial for adolescents to prevent these diseases as they enter their teenage years.

    HPV Vaccine: Protects against human papillomavirus, which can lead to certain cancers. The vaccine is given in a series for optimal protection.

    Meningococcal Vaccine: This vaccine protects against meningitis and bloodstream infections caused by meningococcal bacteria. The first dose is critical for teenagers.

  • 16 Years

    Meningococcal Vaccine: Booster dose to enhance protection against meningitis as children approach adulthood, a crucial time for immunity.
